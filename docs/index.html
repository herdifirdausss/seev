What is Seev?

Computer recipesGo code
Practice walletpretend money
Safety rehearsalstests

The shortest answer

Seev is the invisible machinery behind a possible digital wallet.

It is a learning project. It has no customer phone app, contacts no real bank, and moves no real money.

Why build it?To practice money safety during repeats, crashes, and late answers without risking customers.

Front-door helperreceives requests
Identity helperknows the person
Money-in helperfollows top-ups
Money-out helperfollows withdrawals

One job per helper

No single helper should quietly control identity, every workflow, and the final money record.

The story starts with friendly job names. Their technical service names come later.

Why divide the work?A mistake in one job becomes easier to contain, explain, and repair.

Separate helpers

Each service starts separately and owns one responsibility.

Each also owns a private database. Services communicate through APIs or events, never another service's tables.

The costNetworks can fail and facts can temporarily disagree, so separation requires recovery and reconciliation.

Policies & feeslimits and quotes

The big money book

Ledger owns accounts, balances, postings, holds, fees, policies, reconciliation, reports, and outbox events.

It locks accounts, enforces append-only history, and makes one transaction save money rows and event evidence together.

Non-negotiableEvery posting balances, one operation affects money once, and corrections add history instead of deleting it.
