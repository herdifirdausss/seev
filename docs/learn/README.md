## Realistic time by age

These are walkthrough estimates, not promises. “Engineering” assumes basic
programming knowledge; experience matters more than age for that route.

| Typical reader | Core story | Product end to end | Engineering end to end |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Age 5, with an adult | 8–12 min | Not a realistic independent goal | Not a realistic goal |
| Age 10, with occasional help | 10–20 min | 45–90 min, guided | Not a realistic independent goal |
| Age 15 | 5–10 min | 35–60 min | 3–6 hours with basic programming |
| Age 20 | 5–10 min | 30–50 min | 2–4 hours with basic programming |
| Age 25 or 30 | 5–10 min | 25–45 min | 1.5–3 hours with relevant experience; otherwise 2–4 hours |

For a five-year-old, success means retelling the safety promises, not naming
services. For an engineering reader, success means tracing a claim into code,
data, recovery behavior, and proof.
